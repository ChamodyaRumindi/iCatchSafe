Project Scope

Literature Survey

Entire 1770 km of Sri Lanka’s coastline. Around 8,500,000 actively engage in the seafood and aquaculture industry

Figure 1. Map of the Sri Lankan Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) (Source: Maritime Boundaries Geodatabase, Flanders Marine Institute).

The fisheries sector in Sri Lanka is a vital component of the national economy, providing employment and sustenance for a significant portion of the population. However, the industry is plagued by challenges such as communication and safety issues, primarily due to the lack of a robust communication system that facilitates long-distance connectivity with the shore. This literature review explores various communication systems and technologies that could potentially address these challenges and enhance safety and efficiency in the fisheries sector.

The author, N. Jayapal [1] proposed GPS-based system that are widely used in maritime navigation to provide real-time location information of boats and ships, displayed on GPS maps. These systems are essential for sailors as they enable precise navigation, speed calculation, and location determination, ensuring safe and efficient movement through waterways. The accuracy of GPS systems in providing position, speed, and heading information is essential for the safe arrival of vessels at their destination. While these systems provide valuable navigational aid, they fall short in facilitating real-time communication between vessels and shore, a critical need for addressing safety and illegal fishing concerns.

References

  1. N. Jayapal, "GPS & LabView based system for detection and control of maritime boundary intruding boats," IJERT, Apr 2018.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

There are no records of a smart solution for wireless communication system in Sri Lanka. It is a challenge to provide a solution due to limited data connectivity at the sea.

Severity assessment

A smart solution using LoRa-based wireless communication systems for monitoring fish species and illegal fishing activities has been reported in various regions. However, assessing the severity of fishing violations and the progression of illegal activities has not yet been attempted. Additionally, the implementation of secure communication and real-time weather alerting and forecasting functions remains unexplored.

Information sharing

A real-time communication system using LoRa-based wireless technology has been identified as a priority need for effective information sharing between fishermen, land authorities, and researchers for enhanced secure communication, weather updates and control of illegal fishing activities.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How does the implementation of the sea communication system impact the overall safety and efficiency of the fishery industry in Sri Lanka?

The fishery industry in sri lanka encounters problems such as communication and safety issues because of the lack of a reliable communication system that enables long-distance connection with the shore. The current communication channels are restricted, causing accidents and facilitating illegal fishing activities.


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

The proposed wireless communication system, powered by LoRa technology, is designed to revolutionize communication and safety in the fishery industry. It offers a range of features, including emergency alerts, detection of illegal fishing activities, Sea Weather Prediction, and an Early Warning System. Additionally, integrating blockchain and smart contract technology ensures secure and reliable communication. This approach encrypts data before transmission, ensuring only authorized access, promoting transparency, and enhancing trust within the fishery ecosystem. The system's ability to transmit distress signals to nearby vessels, along with the sender's boat's precise location, will significantly enhance safety and productivity in the fishing sector. The current unreliable communication system in the fishery industry poses significant challenges in identifying illegal fishing activities. The proposed LoRa-based wireless communication system will overcome these barriers by facilitating instant data exchange between fishing vessels and shore stations, enabling the swift identification of illegal fishing activities. Moreover, the system's ability to monitor fishing activities and analyze the data will significantly boost the industry's efficiency. Furthermore, the incorporation of a Sea Weather Prediction and Early Warning System represents a pivotal advancement for the industry. It will allow fishermen to precisely forecast weather conditions and prevent accidents resulting from severe weather. The system can also issue warning signals to mitigate accidents and legal issues, thereby enhancing the safety and security of the fishery business. Employing LoRa-based wireless communication technology, renowned for its low-power consumption and extensive range, the system caters to the specific needs of the fisheries industry through the establishment of an ad hoc network. This network links IoT nodes, ensuring seamless communication between fishing boats and land stations. The proposed LoRa-based wireless communication system offers a comprehensive solution to these challenges. By establishing a robust communication network, this system enables real-time data exchange between fishing vessels and shore stations. This allows fishermen to communicate effectively, receive weather updates, and send distress signals if needed. The system's features, such as emergency alerts and detection of illegal fishing activities, enhance safety measures and help prevent accidents and illegal activities. Overall, the system addresses major issues and provides a way for a more effective and sustainable sector in the fishing industry by enhancing coordination, communication, and safety.

Research Objectives

Implementation of LoRa-based Wireless Communication System

To enhance communication and safety measures for fishermen in Sri Lanka by implementing a LoRa-based wireless communication system that facilitates seamless boat-to-boat and boat-to-shore communication, along with an SOS system for emergencies.

Implementation of Sea Weather Prediction and Early Warning System

mobile-based solution that utilizes IoT technology to monitor weather conditions in real time and provide fishermen with accurate forecasts. The application also incorporates an early warning system that alerts fishermen about potential risks such as storms, high waves, or strong winds.

Implementation of Illegal Fishing Detection System

Fish species identification using image recognition, illegal fishing detection, and alerting to shore authorities.

Implementation of Secure Communication, Robust Mobile App Security, and Obstacle Detection System.

secure and reliable communication channel for fishermen using LoRa modules, extending it from boats to lan based stations. It also focuses on implementing robust security measures in mobile applications used in the fishery industry and developing a centralized dashboard for managing and monitoring fishery activities.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed loRa-Based wireless communication system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. LoRa-based Wireless Communication
  2. Sea Weather Prediction and Early Warning System
  3. Fish Species Identification Using Image Recognition, Illegal Fishing Detection and Alerting system
  4. Secure Communication, Robust Mobile App Security, and Obstacle Detection System.

The Figure 2 system diagram illustrates the operation of a comprehensive communication system designed for marine applications. At its core, the system relies on a boat-mounted microcontroller to collect and process various types of data, including geographical coordinates (longitude and latitude) and sensor readings. In the event of an emergency, the microcontroller also handles the transmission of emergency messages. The processed data is then selectively displayed on the boat's display unit. To ensure secure communication, all data is encrypted before being transmitted via a boat-mounted LoRa (Long Range) module. The encrypted data is then relayed through intermediary LoRa nodes, which serve as communication relays, effectively extending the range of the boat's communication capabilities and forming an Ad hoc network. Ultimately, the encrypted data reaches a shore station, where it can be used to provide crucial functionalities such as emergency alerts, real-time weather updates, detection of illegal fishing activities, and issuing warnings to boats about potential barriers.
